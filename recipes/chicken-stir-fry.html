Chicken Stir-Fry

A plate of chicken stir fry.

This will be a quick and easy recipe to make chicken stir fry at home.

Ingredients Needed

    4 cups of water
    2 cups of white rice
    ⅔ cup of soy sauce
    ¼ cup of brown sugar
    1 tablespoon of cornstarch
    1 tablespoon of minced fresh ginger
    1 tablespoon of minced garlic
    ¼ teaspoon of red pepper flakes
    3 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves, thinly sliced
    2 tablespoons of sesame oil, divided
    1 head of broccoli, broken up into florets
    1 onion, cut into large chunks
    1 cup of sliced carrots
    1 (8 ounce) can of sliced water chestnuts, drained
    1 green bell pepper, cut into matchsticks

Steps

    Step 1: Bring water and rice to a boil in a saucepan over high heat. Reduce heat to medium low, cover, and simmer until rice is tender, and liquid has been absorbed. 20 to 25 minutes
    Step 2: Meanwhile, combine soy sauce, brown sugar and cornstarch in a medium sized bowl. Stir until smooth. Stir in your ginger, garlic and red pepper flakes. Add chicken and stir to coat. Cover and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 15 minutes
    Step 3: Heat 1 tablespoon of sesame oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add in your broccoli, onion, carrots, water chestnuts and bell pepper. Cook and stir until just tender, About 5 minutes. Transfer vegetables into a dish and set aside.
    Step 4: Heat the remaining 1 tablespoon of sesame oil in the same wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add in your chicken, reserving marinade and cook until just browned. About 2 minutes per side. Stir in your vegetables and reserved marinade. Bring to a boil. Cook and stir until chicken is no longer pink in the center and vegetables are tender, 5 to 7 minutes. Serve over rice.
